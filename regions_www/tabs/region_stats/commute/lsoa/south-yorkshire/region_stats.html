Key information for South Yorkshire, LSOA geography

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) for South Yorkshire. The data were generated on 2017-07-05 and this document was created on 2017-07-13. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. The PCT source code can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This version of the PCT uses origin-destination data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The interface is currently displaying the number of commuters travelling by different modes between Lower Super Output Area zones (LSOAs, average size 690 commuters). There were 552,083 commuters living in South Yorkshire recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these commuters are included in the zones data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download.

Adding in inter-regional flows, there were 568,363 commuters living or working in South Yorkshire in the 2011 Census, and commuting in a flow containing at least 3 commuters (in either direction). These commuters are included in the All flows files on the data download pages. Flows containing fewer than 3 commuters are excluded from the visualisation and download files for data protection reasons.

Straight lines, Fast routes and Quieter routes are both visualised on the map interface and available to download. These lines and routes contain all 22,941 flows between LSOAs that a) start or end in South Yorkshire, b) have a fast route distance <20km, and c) contain at least 5 commuters (by any mode, in either direction). These 22,941 between-zone LSOA flows contain 286,404 commuters who either live or work in South Yorkshire.

Between-zone LSOA flows exclude within-LSOA travel, when the LSOA zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-LSOA travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 16,285 commuters living in South Yorkshire. Between-zone flows also exclude those with no fixed place of work.

The Route Network (LSOA) is built from all between-zone LSOA fast routes that a) start or end in South Yorkshire, and b) have a fast route distance <20km. For more details please see User Manual C.

See Lovelace et al. (2017) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and the methods used to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.


The South Yorkshire region

Figure 1 shows a map of South Yorkshire, including the regional boundary (thick black border), local authority boundaries (blue lines) and LSOA zones in the region (grey with a black border). The straight green lines represent the top 100 flows in terms of the number of commuters by any mode.

Figure 1: Map of the South Yorkshire region The left-hand map in Figure 2 shows the proportion of commuters in each zone with a fast route commute distance less than 10km (calculated excluding people with no fixed workplace). The average proportion was 62% across zones in South Yorkshire, compared with a national average of 57%. The right-hand map shows the average hilliness of the fastest routes used by commuters living in each zone. The average was 3.4% across zones in South Yorkshire, compared with a national average of 2.2%.

Cycling levels in each scenario for South Yorkshire

The proportion of commuters who cycled as their main mode of travel to work baseline (2011 Census) data in South Yorkshire was 1.7%, compared to the national average of 3.1% in England and Wales as a whole. The percentage of cycling expected was 2.2%, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England and Wales.

Table 1 summarises the proportion of commuters walking, driving and using other modes under each scenario in South Yorkshire, based on aggregating across all residents in all the LSOA zones.

Health and carbon impacts in South Yorkshire

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.

Table 2 illustrates the health and carbon impacts of the different scenarios in South Yorkshire, based on aggregating across all residents in all the LSOA zones. For each scenario, comparisons are made with a counterfactual in which nobody in the region cycled ('No cyclists'), and with the observed level of cycling in the 2011 Census.
