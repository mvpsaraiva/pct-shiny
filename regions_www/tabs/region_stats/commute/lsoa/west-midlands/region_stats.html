Key information for the West Midlands, LSOA geography

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) for the West Midlands. The data were generated on 2017-07-05 and this document was created on 2017-07-13. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. The PCT source code can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This version of the PCT uses origin-destination data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The interface is currently displaying the number of commuters travelling by different modes between Lower Super Output Area zones (LSOAs, average size 690 commuters). There were 935,511 commuters living in the West Midlands recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these commuters are included in the zones data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download.

Adding in inter-regional flows, there were 981,340 commuters living or working in the West Midlands in the 2011 Census, and commuting in a flow containing at least 3 commuters (in either direction). These commuters are included in the All flows files on the data download pages. Flows containing fewer than 3 commuters are excluded from the visualisation and download files for data protection reasons.

Straight lines, Fast routes and Quieter routes are both visualised on the map interface and available to download. These lines and routes contain all 39,604 flows between LSOAs that a) start or end in the West Midlands, b) have a fast route distance <20km, and c) contain at least 5 commuters (by any mode, in either direction). These 39,604 between-zone LSOA flows contain 413,761 commuters who either live or work in the West Midlands.

Between-zone LSOA flows exclude within-LSOA travel, when the LSOA zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-LSOA travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 18,687 commuters living in the West Midlands. Between-zone flows also exclude those with no fixed place of work.

The Route Network (LSOA) is built from all between-zone LSOA fast routes that a) start or end in the West Midlands, and b) have a fast route distance <20km. For more details please see User Manual C.

See Lovelace et al. (2017) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and the methods used to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.


The West Midlands region

Figure 1 shows a map of the West Midlands, including the regional boundary (thick black border), local authority boundaries (blue lines) and LSOA zones in the region (grey with a black border). The straight green lines represent the top 100 flows in terms of the number of commuters by any mode.

Figure 1: Map of the West Midlands region The left-hand map in Figure 2 shows the proportion of commuters in each zone with a fast route commute distance less than 10km (calculated excluding people with no fixed workplace). The average proportion was 66% across zones in the West Midlands, compared with a national average of 57%. The right-hand map shows the average hilliness of the fastest routes used by commuters living in each zone. The average was 2.4% across zones in the West Midlands, compared with a national average of 2.2%.

Cycling levels in each scenario for the West Midlands

The proportion of commuters who cycled as their main mode of travel to work baseline (2011 Census) data in the West Midlands was 1.7%, compared to the national average of 3.1% in England and Wales as a whole. The percentage of cycling expected was 3%, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England and Wales.

Table 1 summarises the proportion of commuters walking, driving and using other modes under each scenario in the West Midlands, based on aggregating across all residents in all the LSOA zones.

Health and carbon impacts in the West Midlands

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.

Table 2 illustrates the health and carbon impacts of the different scenarios in the West Midlands, based on aggregating across all residents in all the LSOA zones. For each scenario, comparisons are made with a counterfactual in which nobody in the region cycled ('No cyclists'), and with the observed level of cycling in the 2011 Census.
